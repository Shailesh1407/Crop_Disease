Disease Prevention Guide

Best practices to protect your crops from diseases

Before Planting

Soil Testing

Test soil pH and nutrient levels. Adjust soil conditions to optimal ranges for your crop. Healthy soil promotes strong, disease-resistant plants.

Quality Seeds

Use certified, disease-free seeds from reputable suppliers. Treat seeds with fungicides if recommended for your region.

Resistant Varieties

Select crop varieties bred for disease resistance. Check with local extension services for recommended varieties.

Crop Rotation

Plan crop rotation to break disease cycles. Avoid planting the same crop family in the same location consecutively.

During Growing Season

Proper Spacing

Follow recommended plant spacing to ensure good air circulation. Crowded plants create humid microclimates favorable for diseases.

Water Management

Water at the base of plants, not on leaves. Water in the morning to allow foliage to dry. Avoid overwatering.

Pruning

Remove dead, damaged, or diseased branches promptly. Prune to improve air flow and light penetration.

Regular Inspection

Monitor crops weekly for early disease signs. Use CropCare AI for quick diagnosis when symptoms appear.

Treatment Strategies

Organic Methods

  • Neem Oil: Natural fungicide and insecticide, apply every 7-10 days
  • Copper-based Sprays: Effective against bacterial and fungal diseases
  • Compost Tea: Boosts beneficial microbes that suppress pathogens
  • Sulfur: Traditional fungicide for powdery mildew and rusts
  • Baking Soda Solution: Helps control fungal diseases (1 tsp per liter of water)

Chemical Methods

  • Systemic Fungicides: Absorbed by plants, provide longer protection
  • Contact Fungicides: Protect plant surfaces, need regular reapplication
  • Preventive Sprays: Apply before disease appears during high-risk periods
  • Curative Treatments: Applied after infection, may stop disease progression
  • Rotate Products: Prevent resistance by alternating fungicide classes

Seasonal Calendar

Spring

  • Prepare soil and test pH
  • Apply preventive fungicides
  • Monitor for early infections
  • Begin regular inspections

Summer

  • Maintain consistent watering
  • Monitor humidity levels
  • Apply treatments as needed
  • Harvest and remove diseased plants

Fall

  • Clean up crop residues
  • Remove fallen leaves
  • Prepare for next season
  • Plan crop rotation

Winter

  • Sanitize tools and equipment
  • Plan next season's crops
  • Order disease-resistant seeds
  • Review and update practices
