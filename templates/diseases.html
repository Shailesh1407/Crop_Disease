Comprehensive guide to crop diseases detected by CropCare AI

Apple Diseases

Apple Scab

Caused by: Venturia inaequalis

Fungal disease causing dark, velvety lesions on leaves and fruits. Common in humid conditions.

View Details

Black Rot

Caused by: Botryosphaeria obtusa

Fungal disease affecting leaves, fruit, and bark. Causes circular black lesions and fruit rot.

View Details

Cedar Rust

Caused by: Gymnosporangium juniperi-virginianae

Rust disease requiring both apple and cedar trees. Causes orange spots on leaves.

View Details

Corn Diseases

Common Rust

Caused by: Puccinia sorghi

Fungal disease producing reddish-brown pustules on leaves. Can reduce yield significantly.

View Details

Northern Leaf Blight

Caused by: Exserohilum turcicum

Fungal disease causing elongated gray-green lesions. Spreads rapidly in warm, humid weather.

View Details

Cercospora Leaf Spot

Caused by: Cercospora zeae-maydis

Fungal disease causing gray leaf spots. Also known as Gray Leaf Spot.

Disease Prevention Tips

Regular Monitoring

Inspect crops weekly for early signs of disease. Early detection enables timely treatment.

Air Circulation

Ensure proper spacing and pruning to reduce humidity and prevent fungal growth.

Sanitation

Remove and destroy infected plant material. Clean tools between uses.

Resistant Varieties

Choose disease-resistant crop varieties when available to reduce risk.
